Is this the real life, or is this just fantasy?

An evening with Tuggin on Tuggles manager Rich Goldman –

2023 Chicken Nuggets Champion

Special report for ESPN

By Seth Wickersham

January 2, 2023

====================================================================

“If they hate then let em hate, and watch the money pile up.”

                                                                    –50 Cent

====================================================================

I’m sitting with Tuggin on Tuggles manager Rich Goldman at the Thirsty Crow, the divey Patriots bar in the Gaslamp District of San Diego. He’s taking a bite out of his fifth glass of absinthe.

“I had no idea people felt this way about me, it caught me off-guard. It made me tinkle a little bit,” says Rich, AKA Unkle Rikky, AKA Der Fuhrer.

A troubled look comes over his face. “Not that last part,” he says. “Can we take that out?”

We’re talking about the evening of September 7, 2022, Draft Day for Chicken Nuggets. The animosity was palpable.

“So I joined this Zoom call for the draft,” he says, “and people lashed out at me. ‘Rich, your texts are annoying,’ said one commish. ‘SO annoying,’ chirped an East Coast manager. ‘So fecking annoying,’ squeaked someone else. Did we even have an Irish guy in the league? ‘What about me?’ peeped another little man.”

He continues, “‘Ohhh, I have to pay ten cents for each text. Please, stop! I can’t put the subwoofer in my man-cave now! My children cannot go to college! Ohhhh!’”

“Haters gonna hate,” I say.

“Seth,” replies Unkle Rikky, raising his cup, “haters gonna hate. These folks should have a good long look in the mirror.”

If they did, they would see the eye-popping stats of a true champion, assuming they had a browser window open to ESPN on their laptop, and assuming said laptop was located somewhere in the reflection of the mirror. Never mind that Tuggin on Tuggles scored 8th in points overall, they allowed 2nd fewest points overall.

“We’ve always had a defense-first approach,” says Uncle Rikky. “You know, ‘Bend, don’t break.’ All that shit.” He suddenly becomes impatient. “Look, I don’t have time to get technical with laymen. You can score all the points in the world, but if you let the other team score 158 on you, you’re looking at an early playoff exit.”

“You know a lot about fantasy football,” I say.

Der Fuhrer just nods. A little absinthe dribbles down his chin as he looks pensively out the grimy window of the Thirsty Crow.

He continues, “The telling moment was after the draft, when people started critiquing each other’s teams. ‘Oh wow, whosie-who, you have great receivers! But your tight end SUCKS!’ ‘Oh wow, whatsie-what, your team is really nice and balanced! You’re gonna do so well this year!’ These are, like, the best managers in the game, so naturally, I was curious to hear what their razor-sharp fantasy minds had to say. But… they never got to my team, they just skipped over it.”

“Really?” I said, drawing on my inhaler. (Don’t be confused by the name “Wickersham” – it’s Jewish.)

“It didn’t dawn on me until later,” he says, “that nobody critiqued my team because they thought my team sucked ass. Like, beyond critique. Nobody would go near my team with a ten-foot pole. I was like Louis Winthorpe showing up at the squash club.”

“We don’t want your drugs around here, Louis,” I say, “so why don’t you just go away!”

Uncle Rikky nods. “There is something rotten at The Heritage Club.”

Uncle Rikky has goaded me into eating an edible gummy. I think, Why not? I’ve never done it before, because my name is Seth Wickersham, but how bad could it be? He mumbles something about wanting to meet Cousin Sal and asks if I can introduce him. When I ask why, he looks at me like I’m an idiot. “I’m Uncle Rikky, he’s Cousin Sal.”

Suddenly time and space have been disrupted, and we are at Beshock Ramen in SD’s East Village and I am eating eel. Another decidedly un-Seth thing to do. Who are you, Seth? Who the fuck are you? Rabbi Shari Gamliel (my rabbi, who claims to be a descendant of the great Rabbi Gamliel, who once said it would be a good idea to mix bitter herbs with wine) would not approve. I splash some cold water on my face and return to the table.

“For the thousands of team managers reading this right now,” I say, “describe your winning strategy.”

“First and foremost,” he says, “you want to pick really good players. Like, real stars. Like Justin Jefferson. CMC. Try to pick the #1 receiver and the #1 running back like I did. If you can’t get ones, get twos. If you can’t get twos, get threes. And so on and so forth.”

“Wow, I think our readers are really going to appreciate that. And you grabbed Amari for cheap – the other managers didn’t like him.”

“They let me know, too,” he responds. “‘Oh, he stinks!’ they said. ‘Oh, he disappears!’ they said. But he ended up top-12 for the year. And he dropped 27 in the final.”

“What other tips do you have?”

“Well, I guess, find a really good running back for under $25. Like someone you can trade even after he has season-ending surgery. Like Breece Hall.”

“Lucky there,” I say.

“I don’t believe in luck,” says Uncle Rikky, a soba noodle slipping out his nostril.

We’re now shrooming at Double Deuce, with its mechanical bull and SoCal sorority-girl bartenders dancing on TOP of the bars in their cowgirl boots. It’s a redneck Coyote Ugly. I didn’t really want to shroom – I am Seth Wickersham, after all – but it was hard to say no when Uncle Rikky insisted they would enhance the flavor of my shoyu ramen.

Perhaps I get a little too deep when I ask, “What do you think future generations will say about Tuggin on Tuggles? Will they remember the manager, or the players?”

Uncle Rikky seems annoyed by my question and looks down at his phone. “I’ve gotta take this call,” he says and steps out into the lobby.

I feel woozy and unsure of myself, but my spidey sense says to follow him, and so I do. Without my astute investigative instincts, there is no Seth Wickersham. So I follow him into the lobby, and I eavesdrop from behind a fake cactus. Or is it real? I can’t tell. At first, I think it’s fake, but when I grab hold of it, real needles seem to penetrate my hand, and real blood seems to run down my arm. Will have to investigate further, or my name is not Seth Wickersham!

Uncle Rikky is quite animated, chewing on a toothpick – such a Double Deuce thing to do! “Yes, I think he bought the whole story!” he says into his phone. “He’s even eaten all the shrooms and edibles. Haha, what a SUCKER!”

Could he really be saying such things? Or is this just fantasy? I stand up, in a daze, and stumble out from the cacti. I hear voices now – a barrage of questions. Who is Der Fuhrer? Who is Seth Wickersham? WHAT is Seth Wickersham? What is your brand, Seth Wickersham? WHAT IS YOUR FUCKING BRAND?

My foot catches on a cactus branch, and I trip and fall forward. I close my eyes and float downward through the cold, crisp ether with a sense of finality, like a bungee jumper who knows his cable is too long.

When I open my eyes again, I am in my hotel bed and it’s the next morning. I have a headache like I’ve never felt before.

As if on cue, I get a text from Uncle Rikky: “This story is starting to confuse me. Print whatever you want.”

“Even the tinkle part?” I type back.

“Even the tinkle part.”

I look at my arm. No blood. No cactus quills. No bumps or bruises. Looking back on this fateful night, I cannot be sure what was said, nor with what conviction – if any. Despite all this, I have no doubt that I was in the presence of greatness.

–Seth Wickersham, San Diego, January 2, 2023
